Quantitative Researcher and Data Scientist with 8+ years of experience applying Machine Learning, Deep Learning, econometrics, and stochastic modeling to complex systems and financial markets, with a current focus on cryptocurrencies, DeFi, and blockchain. Proven track record in developing end-to-end ML pipelines, deep neural networks (CNNs, autoencoders), and large-scale data-driven solutions across both industry and academia.

Current research (PhD, PUC-Rio): Behavioral Finance and digital asset markets, including investor behavior, market efficiency, and herding dynamics. My work integrates causal inference (Synthetic Difference-in-Differences), asset pricing models, and large-scale ML analysis across 1,000+ cryptocurrencies, combining statistical rigor with AI-driven insights.

Publications: Author of multiple international peer-reviewed papers, including in Economics Letters (Elsevier) and Finance Research Letters. My research demonstrates how political shocks and macro events generate structural breaks, market segmentation, and behavioral inefficiencies in crypto markets. Additional work applies Deep Learning (e.g., denoising autoencoders, CNNs) and Machine Learning to signal extraction, classification, and pattern recognition in noisy environments.

Background: Physics (UFF) + M.Sc. in Mathematical Modeling (UFRJ/COPPE), providing a strong quantitative and computational foundation. Professional experience spans geophysics, energy, and finance, applying AI methods (NLP, Deep Learning, large-scale data processing) to real-world problems, including seismic data interpretation and blockchain analytics.

Core expertise:

• Machine Learning & Deep Learning (TensorFlow, Keras, Scikit-learn, CNNs, Autoencoders, NLP)

• Behavioral signals and market inefficiencies in crypto

• Liquidity pools & AMMs 

• Market microstructure & price discovery

• Time series, stochastic processes, and simulation 

• Blockchain analytics & DeFi systems
